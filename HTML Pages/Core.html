Core Workout

The perfect abs workout focuses not only on the six-pack muscles (rectus abdominis) but also the obliques, transverse abdominis, and serratus. To effectively train your core, you need a variety of exercises targeting all these muscles and their full range of functions in the right sequence.

One common mistake in ab training is doing too many top-down crunches, which limits the effectiveness of other exercises like lower ab or rotational movements. Follow the correct sequence to maximize energy and performance:

  • Start with lower ab movements (leg towards ribcage).
  • Incorporate rotation into lower ab exercises.
  • Focus on rotational oblique movements.
  • Include midrange exercises with both top and bottom halves moving together.
  • Finish with top-down and top-down rotational exercises.

Don't forget the serratus, a key muscle for trunk stability and shoulder blade control. The perfect ab workout should also include anti-rotational, anti-lateral, and anti-extension exercises, as well as explosive movements to build athleticism.

Workout for Beginners

  1. ISO Reverse Crunches: 30-60 seconds
  2. Seated Ab Circles: 30-60 seconds (alternate clockwise and counterclockwise)
  3. REST 30 SECONDS
  4. Recliner Elbow to Knee Tucks: 30-60 seconds
  5. Opposite Side Tuck Planks: 30-60 seconds
  6. REST 30 SECONDS
  7. Oak Tree Stepouts: 30-60 seconds each side
  8. Banded Pulldowns: 30-60 seconds
  9. Plank Pushaways: 30-60 seconds

Workout for Advanced Lifters

  1. Hanging ‘X’ Raises: 30-60 seconds
  2. Hanging Leg Spirals: 30-60 seconds (alternate right and left twists)
  3. REST 20 SECONDS
  4. Tornado Chops: 30-60 seconds (alternate right and left chops)
  5. Opposite Scissor V-Ups: 30-60 seconds
  6. REST 20 SECONDS
  7. Sledgehammer Swings: 30-60 seconds each side
  8. Banded Pulldowns: 30-60 seconds
  9. Plank Punchouts: 30-60 seconds

By following this sequence, you’ll target every function of your core muscles for maximum results.
