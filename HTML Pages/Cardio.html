Full Body Circuit Workout

Type: Circuit Training

Focus: Build Muscle, Burn Fat, Lose Weight

Equipment: Medium-to-Heavy Dumbbells (suggested weight: 8-20 lbs)

Frequency: 1-2 times per week

Trainer: Lindsey Bomgren (YouTube Guided Workout)

Workout Overview

This circuit training workout is designed to improve total body strength, build muscle, and burn fat—all from the comfort of your home. The session includes two circuits, each with a mix of strength and cardio exercises, followed by a time drop format for maximum intensity.

Workout Structure:

  • 2 Circuits (4 exercises per circuit)
  • Time Drop Format:
    • Set 1: 40 seconds work, 20 seconds rest
    • Set 2: 30 seconds work, 15 seconds rest
    • Set 3: 20 seconds work, 10 seconds rest
  • Repeat Each Circuit 3x

Workout Outline

Circuit One: Legs

  • Strength: 2-Pulse Squat and Calf Raise
  • Cardio: Double Squat Jump
  • Strength: 2-Pulse Reverse Lunge Thrusters
  • Cardio: Double Lunge Jump

Circuit Two: Arms

  • Strength: 2 Bicep Curls and 2 Standing Chest Flys
  • Cardio: Push Press
  • Strength: 3-Way Back Row
  • Cardio: 4 Mountain Climbers and 1 Push Up

Exercise Descriptions

1. 2-Pulse Squat and Calf Raise

Targets: Legs, glutes, quads, hamstrings, hip flexors, calves, and core.

How to Perform:

  1. Start standing with feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height (front-rack position).
  2. Lower into a squat, pause at the bottom, perform a two-pulse squat (raising and lowering an inch), and then stand up.
  3. As you stand tall, rise onto your toes for a calf raise.
  4. Lower back to starting position and repeat.

2. Double Squat Jump

Targets: Legs, glutes, quads, hamstrings, and core.

How to Perform:

  1. Start in a loaded squat stance, core engaged.
  2. Perform a small squat jump, followed immediately by a more powerful squat jump.
  3. Land softly, and repeat the sequence.

3. 2-Pulse Reverse Lunge Thrusters

Targets: Legs, shoulders, glutes, hamstrings, and core.

How to Perform:

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding dumbbells at your shoulders (front-rack position).
  2. Step back into a reverse lunge, perform a two-pulse movement at the bottom.
  3. As you stand, press the dumbbells overhead.
  4. Repeat on the opposite leg.

4. Double Lunge Jump

Targets: Legs, glutes, hamstrings, and quads.

How to Perform:

  1. Start in a reverse lunge position with one leg back and one forward.
  2. Perform a small jump, maintaining the lunge position.
  3. Explode up, switching legs mid-air.
  4. Land softly and repeat.

5. 2 Bicep Curls and 2 Standing Chest Flys

Targets: Biceps, chest, and shoulders.

How to Perform:

  1. Perform two bicep curls with dumbbells, keeping elbows close to your body.
  2. Transition into two standing chest flys, crossing one arm over your chest at a 45-degree angle.
  3. Repeat the sequence.

6. Push Press

Targets: Shoulders, arms, and core.

How to Perform:

  1. Start standing with feet shoulder-width apart, dumbbells at shoulder height.
  2. Bend slightly at the knees and drive the dumbbells overhead with an explosive push.
  3. Lower back down to shoulder height and repeat.

7. 3-Way Back Row

Targets: Upper and mid-back, lats, and biceps.

How to Perform:

  1. Perform a reverse grip row by pulling dumbbells toward your hips.
  2. Transition to a wide row, pulling elbows out to the sides.
  3. Finish with a narrow row, pulling dumbbells toward your ribcage.

8. 4 Mountain Climbers and 1 Push Up

Targets: Full body, including arms, chest, core, legs, and shoulders.

How to Perform:

  1. Perform four mountain climbers, alternating knees toward the opposite elbow.
  2. After four reps, transition into a push-up, lowering your chest to the ground.
  3. Repeat the sequence.
