Back Workout

The perfect back workout should consist of exercises that target not only the lats and traps but also other important muscles of the back. In order to build a well-rounded back, you need to fill in the gaps left by popular back exercises and incorporate science into your training. That’s what we aim for in this workout.

One major issue with lat-focused training is that it only targets a fraction of what the back muscles can do. Many people perform repetitive exercises that work the back in the same plane. For instance, pullups and lat pulldowns are both vertical pulling exercises. While useful, relying solely on these movements neglects the importance of horizontal pulling.

Even when performing pullups, people often treat it as a high-rep, bodyweight exercise and overlook its potential when weighted. The weighted chin-up, for example, is a powerful compound exercise capable of building significant back strength and size. Additionally, sticking to a single grip limits the development of other back muscles like the teres major, a large muscle located above the lats.

To avoid these issues, start your workout with a foundational strength exercise like the deadlift. The deadlift engages the traps, lats, and lower back all at once, making it a powerhouse exercise for back development. Done with heavy weights and proper form, it can lead to tremendous back gains.

Incorporating straight-arm pushdowns with each warmup set of deadlifts helps reinforce one of the most important movement patterns needed not only for the deadlift but also for all advanced back exercises.

As you progress through the workout, don't forget to train explosively. Following heavy weight training with speed-focused movements like the barbell dead row is essential for building back strength and size. The dead row helps you accelerate heavier weights, improving your overall performance.

To focus on the lats, choose one of the following exercises: alternating 1-arm high cable rows or rocking pulldowns. Both options allow you to develop the mind-muscle connection necessary for maximum hypertrophy of the lats. Focus on squeezing each rep and taking the lats through their full range of motion.

Finally, no back workout is complete without corrective exercises. Be sure to target both the lumbar erectors and the rotator cuff. The Hyper Y/W combo is perfect for this, engaging both muscle groups with light weights. This exercise helps with stabilization and overall back health.

How to Construct the Perfect Back Workout

  1. Superset 1:
    • 1A. Deadlift: 10 reps, 8 reps
    • 1B. Weighted Chin-up: 4RM, 8RM

  2. Superset 2:
    • 2A. Deadlift: 6 reps, 6 reps
    • 2B. Bodyweight Wide-Grip Pullups: To failure (F), repeat

  3. Barbell Dead Rows (12RM):
    • 2-3 sets of 8-10 reps

  4. Alternating 1-Arm High Cable Row OR Rocking Pulldown:
    • 2-3 sets of 10-12 reps

  5. Hyper Y/W Combo:
    • 2 sets of 14-20 reps (alternate Y/W each rep)

  6. Barbell Ladder Shrug Finisher:
    • 1 set to failure (F)

By following this structure, you hit the back through its full range of motion and target all the major muscles in the posterior chain. The supersets and ladder-style exercises increase workout intensity and ensure you create enough overload to stimulate muscle growth.
