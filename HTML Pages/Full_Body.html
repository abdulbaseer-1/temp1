Full Body Workout

If you want to try just a single total body workout routine, especially if you're new to this type of training, start with the A workout. If you're aiming for a more rigorous routine, alternate between A and B workouts on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.

Here are some key benefits of total body workouts:

  • Focus on bigger muscles using compound exercises.
  • Increase strength and improve functional performance.
  • Stimulate muscle growth every 48 hours for maximum gains.
  • Achieve more frequent protein synthesis and training volume.

In this total body split, the focus is on specific movements, such as:

  • Lower Body: Lunge, Hinge, Squat
  • Upper Body: Push, Pull
  • Core: Corrective, Carry

Workout A

  1. 3D Lunge Warmup: 2-3 sets, 7 reps each direction
  2. Barbell Squats: 3 sets of 5
  3. Barbell Hip Thrusts: 3-4 sets of 10-12
  4. Barbell Bench Press: 3 sets of 5
  5. Weighted Chin-Ups: 3 sets of 6-10 to failure
  6. Dumbbell Farmer’s Carries: 3-4 sets of 50 steps (half bodyweight)
  7. Face Pulls: 2 sets of 12 (with 12 sets of 1 mentality)

Workout B

  1. 3D Lunge Warmup: 2-3 sets, 7 reps each direction
  2. Deadlifts: 3 sets of 5
  3. Barbell Squats or Reverse Lunges: 3-4 sets of 10
  4. Barbell OHP (Overhead Press): 3 sets of 5
  5. Barbell Rows: 3 sets of 10-12
  6. Dumbbell Overhead Farmer’s Carries: 3-4 sets of 50 steps (one-quarter bodyweight)
  7. Face Pulls or other corrective exercises: 2 sets of 12

For beginners, start with Workout A. If you're ready for more, alternate between A and B, three times a week for maximum results.
